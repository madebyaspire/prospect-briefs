  • Blog cadence is exceptional — 27+ posts in 14 months, roughly biweekly, covering topics that enterprise technology buyers actually care about. This is a rare asset most consultancies never build

Where the Gaps Are

  • WordPress.com hosting is a credibility problem — The site runs on wordpress.com (free/premium tier), not self-hosted WordPress. For a technology consultancy, this is like a financial advisor running their practice from a Gmail address. The WordPress.com toolbar and login prompts are visible. It signals "blog" not "business"
  • No About page — tellaire.com/about returns a 404. Who runs Tellaire? What's their background? For B2B advisory services, the people ARE the product. This is a critical trust gap

  • Contact page is a bare form — Name, email, message. No phone number, no address, no scheduling link. For a company selling six-figure advisory engagements, this is a conversion wall
  • Homepage is all rotating hero banners — The four service pillars cycle through full-screen hero slides with "Learn More" buttons. This is a 2015 design pattern. Modern B2B sites use scannable above-the-fold layouts that let visitors self-select their path immediately
  • No team/leadership page — The blog is written by "jooserklee" with a Gravatar avatar. No bio, no LinkedIn link, no headshot on the site. Advisory work is sold on expertise and credibility — invisible leadership is a conversion killer
  • Stock photography throughout — Every page uses Unsplash stock images (credited in the footer). While well-chosen, they don't build the kind of trust that photos of the actual team, actual work, or actual client environments would
  • No industry-specific landing pages — LinkedIn says Tellaire serves financial services, retail, healthcare, life sciences, and education. The website has zero industry pages. Each of these verticals should have a dedicated page with relevant case studies and pain points

How AI Search Engines Would Handle Tellaire Today

If a CTO asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "Who are good AI consulting firms for mid-market companies?", Tellaire is unlikely to appear. Here's why:

  • No structured data means no machine-readable identity. AI search engines use schema markup (Organization, ProfessionalService, Article) to understand what a business does, who runs it, and what it offers. Tellaire has none. The site is readable by humans but essentially invisible to machines trying to categorize and cite it
  • WordPress.com limits crawlability. Free/premium WordPress.com sites have restricted robots.txt control and limited ability to implement custom schema, JSON-LD, or canonical URL strategies. AI crawlers may deprioritize or skip content they can't fully parse
  • No author authority signals. Blog posts are attributed to "jooserklee" with no linked bio, no LinkedIn profile, no credentials page. AI engines increasingly weight authorship signals — who wrote this, what's their background, are they cited elsewhere? Without these signals, even excellent content gets treated as generic
  • The AIX framework isn't extractable. Tellaire's AI Execution Framework (Scan → Pilot → Scale) is the kind of proprietary methodology that AI engines love to cite — it's structured, actionable, and original. But it's buried inside a service page with no dedicated URL, no schema markup, and no FAQ structure. An AI engine can't cite what it can't cleanly extract

What's Missing — And What Would Fix It

Schema Markup Implementation

Add Organization, ProfessionalService, Article, FAQ, and Person schema across the site. This tells AI engines exactly what Tellaire does, who the team is, what services are offered, and how to cite each piece of content. This is the single highest-leverage technical change for AI search visibility.

Critical for AI Search

Author Entity Building

Create a detailed author page with credentials, experience, LinkedIn link, conference appearances, and published works. Link every blog post to this author entity. AI engines use author reputation as a ranking signal — an established author entity makes every piece of content more citable.

High Impact

FAQ + How-To Structured Content

Restructure key blog posts and service pages with FAQ schema. Questions like "What does an AI strategy engagement cost?", "How long does an AI pilot take?", and "What's the difference between AI consulting and AI implementation?" are exactly what AI search engines surface as direct answers.

Medium Impact Quick Win

Voice Search Optimization

Voice queries are conversational: "Who can help my company implement AI?" rather than "AI consulting firm Ohio." Tellaire's blog content is already conversational in tone — but it needs to be structured with question-and-answer patterns, featured snippet formatting, and concise summary paragraphs that voice assistants can read aloud.

Medium Impact Ongoing
The opportunity is timing. Most B2B consultancies haven't optimized for AI search yet — they're still thinking in terms of traditional SEO. Tellaire has a narrow window to be among the first technology advisory firms that AI engines can cleanly parse, cite, and recommend. The content already exists. The structure just needs to catch up.

06Here's What We'd Actually Do

Tellaire doesn't need a website redesign — it needs a website that actually functions as a business development platform. The content is strong. The positioning is clear. The packaging needs to match.

1. Platform Migration: WordPress.com → Self-Hosted

Move from WordPress.com to a self-hosted solution (Next.js, Astro, or self-hosted WordPress on quality hosting). This eliminates the WordPress.com toolbar and branding, gives full control over SEO, allows custom schema markup, and removes the credibility gap of a managed blog platform. All 27+ blog posts migrate with full URL preservation. This single change unlocks everything else.

Critical 3-4 weeks

2. Build the Trust Pages

The site is missing the pages that B2B buyers check before making contact: About (team bios, company story, values), individual case study pages with full metrics and methodology, industry-specific pages for each vertical Tellaire serves, and a "How We Work" page that explains engagement models and provides pricing signals. Every one of these pages exists as content in Tellaire's head — it just hasn't been written for the web yet.

High Impact 2-3 weeks

3. Conversion Architecture

Replace the bare contact form with a real conversion system: a scheduling link (Calendly or similar) on every service page, a "Request a Discovery Call" CTA in the site header, case study downloads gated behind email capture, and an email nurture sequence for leads who aren't ready to talk yet. The current site has one conversion path (contact form) with no urgency, no specificity, and no alternative actions.

High Impact Quick Win

4. SEO Foundation + Blog Optimization

Implement comprehensive schema markup (Organization, ProfessionalService, Article, FAQ), optimize existing blog posts with proper metadata and internal linking, create pillar pages around Tellaire's core topics (AI governance, enterprise architecture, tech debt), and establish the technical SEO foundation that lets the existing content rank for the searches it already deserves.

High Impact Ongoing

5. AIX Framework as a Standalone Asset

The Tellaire AI Execution Framework (Scan, Pilot, Scale) is genuine intellectual property. It deserves its own dedicated, beautifully designed page — not buried in a service page. Build it as a linkable, shareable resource with downloadable components. This becomes a backlink magnet, a sales tool, and a differentiation asset all at once.
