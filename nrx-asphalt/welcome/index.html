05The Future of Search

Search is no longer just Google's ten blue links. AI-powered answers, voice queries, and "zero-click" results increasingly shape how buyers find contractors before they ever visit a website. Here's where NRX stands — and where Phase 2 needs to take the site.

Phase 1 Search Readiness

The current HTML site has solid page-level basics: title tags, meta descriptions, and readable heading structure. But it has no structured data markup — no JSON-LD schema that tells search engines (and AI systems like Google's SGE or ChatGPT) what kind of business NRX is, what services they offer, where they operate, or how to contact them. This is the most significant technical gap.

Why schema markup matters now more than ever: When someone asks Google's AI Overview "who does commercial parking lot paving near Delaware, Ohio," the AI synthesizes answers from pages with structured data it can parse. A business without schema markup is invisible to these AI-generated answers — even if it has a perfectly readable website. Phase 2 fixes this directly.

What Phase 2 Will Add

  • LocalBusiness schema — Confirms NRX's name, address, phone, and service area to all search engines and AI systems
  • Service schema — Tags each of NRX's six service lines in a machine-readable format that powers rich search results
  • Review schema — Once reviews are collected, surfaces star ratings directly in search results
  • FAQ schema — Common contractor questions ("Do you do residential work?" "What areas do you serve?") answered in structured format for featured snippet eligibility
  • BreadcrumbList schema — Helps search engines understand the site's hierarchy and surface the right page for each query

Voice Search and "Near Me" Queries

Voice search queries — "Hey Google, find me an asphalt contractor near me" — are growing among field-based buyers. A site manager standing in a parking lot doesn't type. They ask. Responding to voice queries requires clean local business data, clear service descriptions, and schema markup. All three are Phase 2 deliverables.

E-E-A-T Positioning (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

NRX has extraordinary E-E-A-T raw material: 30 years of experience, a named founder with a personal values story, safety certifications, and real project history. The problem is that none of this is currently surfaced in formats that search algorithms recognize. Phase 2 will explicitly build the content structures — About page depth, team credentials, project case study pages, and safety documentation — that communicate E-E-A-T to search engines.
