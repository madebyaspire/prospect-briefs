01The Honest Picture

BKM Construction, LLC has been a fixture in Delaware, Ohio since May 1989. Founded and still run by Brad Kelley, the company has spent 36 years doing the kind of work that holds up — concrete flatwork, asphalt cutting, mud jacking, compaction grouting, foundation work, framing, remodeling, and a wide range of construction and repair services. Brad built this company with his hands and his reputation, and both are solid.

What BKM does, they do quietly and well. Four verified Google reviews, all 5 stars. Customers say things like "Very responsive, on time, and on budget" and "These guys are the concrete experts in Delaware — they use tried and true old school ways of getting the job done." One reviewer had a concrete patio installed in 2023 and came back a year later to note it still looked exactly the same as the day they left. That's the kind of review that wins jobs. Brad's operation is also a member of the Columbus Chamber of Commerce, listed across construction directories, and has a BBB file that's been open since 2020 with zero complaints.

The services list is genuinely wide — from concrete construction, stamped concrete, and slab leveling to barn construction, fire and water damage remediation, kitchen and bathroom remodeling, deck construction, and accessibility/disability remodeling. This isn't just a concrete company. It's a full-service construction operation that happens to be run like one — quietly, without much fanfare, and primarily by word of mouth.

Here's the honest problem: BKM is practically invisible online. The domain bkmconstruction.com is not owned by Brad — it's listed for sale at $3,995 on HugeDomains. There is no dedicated company website. Their online presence consists of a Chamber listing, a BBB profile, and a handful of directory entries that mostly show the same boilerplate. In a market where homeowners and property managers start their contractor search with a Google search, BKM is missing before they even begin.

05The Future of Search

Search behavior is shifting fast. Google's AI Overviews now answer questions directly in the search results. Voice assistants handle "best concrete contractor near me" queries. And platforms like ChatGPT increasingly recommend local service providers. For BKM Construction, this creates an urgent baseline problem — and a clean opportunity.

The AI Search Problem for BKM

When someone asks Google's AI "Who are the best concrete contractors in Delaware, Ohio?", the AI pulls from structured, indexed, authoritative sources. Right now, BKM's best representation in any indexed source is a Chamber of Commerce listing and a BBB profile. There's no website, no structured data, no schema markup, no FAQ content. AI systems won't recommend a business they can't learn anything about.

What AI Search Looks For

  • Google Business Profile completeness — Services listed, photos uploaded, Q&A answered, posts active. BKM's GBP appears minimal, which limits AI visibility even though 4 excellent reviews exist.
  • Schema markup on a website — Structured data (LocalBusiness, Contractor, Service schema) tells AI systems exactly what BKM does, where they serve, and how to contact them. Without a website, this doesn't exist.
  • E-E-A-T signals — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Brad's 36 years are a gold mine of E-E-A-T content — but none of it is written down anywhere Google can read. A well-written About page alone would be transformative.
  • Review quantity and recency — Four 5-star reviews is a great foundation, but volume matters for AI recommendations. Competitors with 100+ reviews will be surfaced preferentially. Building that review count is a key growth lever.

Voice Search Readiness

Voice searches for contractors tend to be high-intent: "Find a concrete contractor open near me," "Who does mud jacking in Delaware Ohio?" These queries pull directly from Google Business Profile data and structured website content. BKM can't capture any of this traffic without both a GBP and a website working together.

The silver lining: BKM is starting from zero, which means there's no legacy mess to clean up. A well-built website with proper schema, a fully optimized GBP, and a consistent review-building strategy could move BKM from invisible to visible within 90 days of launch. The path is clear.
