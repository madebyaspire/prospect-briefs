01The Honest Picture

Restoration Brew Worx isn't just a brewery — it's a Delaware institution. Your taproom currently pours 12 house-brewed beers — from the crisp Delicious Witbier and the locally inspired Sulky German Pilsner (a nod to Delaware's Little Brown Jug Pacer Horse Race) to the bold 11.2% ABV Silverback Russian Imperial Stout. Your Untappd presence shows an engaged audience that loves tracking your rotating taps. Reviewers consistently praise the generous portions, creative beer menu, and the warm, welcoming vibe. The challenge? Your digital presence hasn't kept pace with what you've built inside those four walls. Your food page links to a PDF menu rather than displaying it natively. And your TripAdvisor listing is still unclaimed. These are fixable things — and fixing them could meaningfully grow your reach.

05The Future of Search

Search is changing fast. Google's AI Overviews, voice assistants, and platforms like ChatGPT are increasingly answering questions directly — without users ever clicking a website link. For a local brewery like Restoration Brew Worx, this shift is both a challenge and an opportunity.

What AI Search Looks For

When someone asks their phone "What's the best brewery in Delaware, Ohio?" or "Where can I get craft beer near me?", AI systems pull from a few key sources:

  • Structured data (schema markup) — Machine-readable information about your business type, hours, menu, location, and reviews. Your site currently has none.
  • Google Business Profile — Your GBP is active, but maximizing it (more photos, Q&A responses, regular posts, complete attributes) directly feeds AI answers.
  • Review volume and sentiment — This is your strongest asset. 263+ reviews with positive sentiment across multiple platforms gives AI confidence in recommending you.
  • E-E-A-T signals — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Google wants to see that real humans with real expertise are behind the content. Your story (Brew Master Frank Barickman, 10+ years of operation, Brewers Association membership) is exactly what builds E-E-A-T — but none of it is on your website in a structured way.

Voice Search Readiness

Voice searches tend to be conversational: "Hey Google, is there a brewery open on Sunday in Delaware, Ohio?" or "What's on tap at Restoration Brew Worx?" Right now, your website can't answer either of these questions in a way voice assistants can parse. Your hours are in all-caps text with no semantic markup, and your beer menu lives on a separate Untappd-powered page that voice assistants can't easily read.

What's Missing

  • Zero schema markup — No Restaurant, Brewery, LocalBusiness, Menu, or Event schema. This is the single biggest technical gap.
  • No FAQ content — "People also ask" questions like "Does Restoration Brew Worx serve food?", "Is Restoration Brew Worx kid friendly?", "Do they have parking?" are all opportunities for featured snippets you're not capturing.
  • No structured event data — If you're doing comedy nights and bourbon samplers, that event data should be marked up so Google can surface it in "things to do in Delaware Ohio" results.
  • Untappd dependency — While Untappd powers your beer menu (which is great for beer enthusiasts), that content lives on Untappd's domain, not yours. Google gives the SEO credit to Untappd, not restorationbrewworx.com.
The bottom line: AI-powered search rewards businesses that make their information easy to find, easy to verify, and easy to trust. Your reviews do the trust part beautifully. A modern website with proper schema, structured content, and an FAQ section would handle the rest — and could be the difference between "We recommend Restoration Brew Worx" and being left out of the AI answer entirely.
