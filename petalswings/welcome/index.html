01The Honest Picture

About a decade ago, Bonnie planted her first garden of irises to share with family and friends. What started as a few rhizomes and a genuine love for the bloom grew into something she hadn't expected: a small business with thousands of happy customers, a presence on Etsy, eBay, and Facebook, and a reputation built the old-fashioned way — by sending beautiful, hand-selected plants to people who trusted her.

PetalsWings is a family iris farm. Every rhizome ships from Bonnie's garden. With over 660 varieties — 82% of them Tall Bearded Iris — she's cultivated one of the most diverse iris catalogs available to home gardeners online. Categories span Tall Bearded, Reblooming, Dwarf, Japanese, Fragrant, Award Winners, and Lily Bulbs. Prices run from $8.99 to $24.99 per bulb, with varieties introduced as far back as 1936 and as recently as last season.

When Bonnie moved from the Etsy/eBay era into her own Shopify store, it was a meaningful step. It gave her control over her catalog and checkout. But the Shopify Dawn theme she was using — functional as it is — couldn't tell her story. There was no brand, no voice, no sense of the decade of growing behind every item listed. The products were good. The digital presence wasn't keeping up.

That's what this rebuild is about. Not replacing what Bonnie does in Shopify — she still manages all 660+ products there, and that stays exactly the same. What changes is everything the customer sees: a custom-designed front end built on Astro 6, deployed to Cloudflare's edge, connected to Shopify via the Storefront API. A site that grows with the season and feels like the farm behind it.

05The Future of Search

Search is changing faster than most small businesses realize. AI-powered results on Google, ChatGPT's shopping recommendations, and voice search on mobile are all drawing from the same underlying signal: E-E-A-T — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. The businesses that win in AI-era search are the ones that have clearly demonstrated real expertise, real history, and real customer relationships.

What AI Search Looks For

  • Structured product data (JSON-LD). AI shopping surfaces pull from Product schema to understand what's being sold, at what price, with what availability. The previous Shopify theme had minimal schema output.
  • Author credibility signals. When Bonnie's name and story appear in structured content — an About page, a blog with a consistent author, a care guide signed by someone with 20 years of experience — that's E-E-A-T in practice.
  • Editorial depth. A growing guide for Tall Bearded Irises written from genuine experience is exactly what AI summarization models prefer over thin product pages. The new site has a full Growing Guides section.
  • Authentic reviews. PetalsWings has thousands of real Etsy and eBay reviews. Migrating that social proof — even as quoted testimonials — gives search engines and AI models clear authority signals.

A complete design system built from PetalsWings's logo and botanical palette. Primary purple (#7B5EA7), soft lavender (#C4A8D8), sage green (#8B9E7C), and warm white. Lora serif headings with DM Sans body copy. Every element designed to feel like Bonnie's farm, not a generic template.

A shop page built for 660+ varieties. Filter chips by type (Tall Bearded, Reblooming, Dwarf, Japanese, and more), sort by price or popularity, and an interactive "Help Me Choose" quiz that guides new iris buyers to the right varieties for their growing conditions and style preferences.

Complete

Preact Cart Islands

Add to Cart, variant selection, and a full cart drawer — all built as interactive Preact islands. Zero full-page reloads for the shopping experience. Cart state is shared across components via nanostores, wired to Shopify checkout and Shop Pay for a seamless transaction flow.

Complete

Dynamic routes for every product — real Shopify data, image galleries, a growing spec bar (type, height, bloom season, sun requirements, hardiness zones, deer resistance), Bonnie's personal care notes surfaced as callouts, and a "Pairs Well With" section no competitor offers.

A full About page built around Bonnie's origin story: the post-9/11 iris garden, 20 years of growing, the family that helps ship every order. Visual timeline, three harvest photos, stats bar (700+ varieties, 20,000+ orders, 5-star rating). A "Meet the Grower" section with Bonnie's actual photo.

09What This Could Mean

We won't fabricate numbers. But the mechanics of how a better product experience compounds into better revenue are worth naming clearly — especially for a business like PetalsWings where the products are excellent and the limiting factor has been the digital experience, not the inventory.

The Core Mechanic
Better Discovery
→ Larger Cart Sizes → More Repeat Buyers → Compounding Seasonal Revenue

Conversion — From Visitor to Buyer

A generic Shopify Dawn template communicates "I have products for sale." A custom-designed brand communicates "I'm the expert you've been looking for." That shift in first impression changes conversion rates. When a visitor lands on a homepage that tells Bonnie's story, shows her farm, and guides them to the right variety for their growing zone — they're more likely to buy, and they're more likely to buy multiple items.

Cart Size — From One Rhizome to a Collection

The "Pairs Well With" section on every Product Detail Page is a deliberate cart-building feature. When a buyer looks at Immortality (TB, white, early bloomer), the page suggests fragrant companions and complementary bloom seasons. Longfield Gardens and Schreiner's don't offer this. No competitor does. A shopper who adds one companion variety to their cart alongside their original pick has materially changed their order size.

Retention — Seasonal Buyers Become Community Members

Iris buyers are inherently seasonal — they plant in spring and fall. The email capture in the newsletter section, combined with seasonal growing guides and variety announcements, turns one-time buyers into annual customers. The loyalty program architecture extends this further. A customer who orders from PetalsWings three times a year for five years is worth significantly more than the same customer acquired once and never retained.

Search — Organic Traffic From Growing Guides

The Growing Guides section doesn't just serve customers who are already on the site. It creates pages that rank for search terms like "how to plant bearded iris" and "reblooming iris care" — terms that get searched by gardeners who haven't heard of PetalsWings yet. When a growing guide ranks and a reader clicks through, the same site that answered their question also has the product for sale. That's the flywheel.

Even a modest improvement compounds quickly. A ten-to-twenty percent lift in conversion rate or average order size — achievable when product discovery is easier and the brand communicates genuine expertise — compounds significantly across two selling seasons per year. The technical infrastructure and brand foundation are built. The lift begins at launch.
