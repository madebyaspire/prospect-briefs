  • The messaging is strong. "Built by Hand. Built to Last." is a genuinely compelling tagline that differentiates you from every prefab cabinet installer out there.

Where the Gaps Are

  • Single page, no depth. There's no separate page for kitchens vs. bathrooms, no project case studies, no FAQ, no blog content. Each of those would be a new opportunity to rank for search terms.

The honest assessment: Your website has good bones — the messaging and brand positioning are strong. But the platform is holding you back. Google Sites simply cannot support the kind of SEO, portfolio showcase, and lead capture that a premium carpentry business needs to compete online.

05The Future of Search

Search is changing fast. Google's AI Overviews, voice assistants, and tools like ChatGPT are increasingly how people discover local businesses. Here's what that means for Keeler Carpentry:

What AI Search Looks For

  • E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). Google's quality guidelines now explicitly reward content from people with first-hand experience. Chad's 25 years of hands-on craftsmanship is exactly the kind of "Experience" signal that AI search prioritizes — but only if it's documented on a website that Google can read.
  • Structured data (schema markup). When someone asks Alexa "who's the best custom cabinet maker in Delaware Ohio?", the answer comes from structured data — JSON-LD markup that tells search engines your business name, location, services, and reviews in a machine-readable format. Your Google Sites page has none of this.
  • Rich, authoritative content. AI search pulls answers from pages with depth. A 200-word homepage can't compete with a competitor who has 50 pages of project case studies, FAQs, and educational content.

What's Missing

  • Zero schema markup. Google Sites doesn't support LocalBusiness, Service, or Review schema. AI search tools literally cannot "see" your business in structured data.
  • No FAQ content. Voice search queries like "how long does a custom kitchen remodel take?" need a page with that exact question answered clearly. You don't have one.
  • No review integration. Your BBB review and any Google reviews aren't connected to your website in a way that search engines can aggregate.
  • No author/expertise signals. There's no "About Chad" page that demonstrates first-hand expertise — the kind of content AI search is specifically designed to reward.
Looking ahead: The businesses that invest in structured data, expertise signals, and rich content now will dominate the next generation of search. For a craftsman with your credentials, this is an enormous advantage waiting to be unlocked.
