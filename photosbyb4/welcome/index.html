01Betsy's Story

Betsy Belote has been photographing the people of Delaware, Ohio for years — and the evidence is in the work itself. Her gallery holds over 140 images spanning three core disciplines: youth and varsity sports, senior portraits, and family sessions. That range matters. It means she's not a one-season shooter waiting for graduation weekend. She's out on sidelines in the fall, in open fields for golden-hour portraits in spring, and on back porches with families year-round.

Sports photography is her deepest catalog — 65 images covering baseball, basketball, soccer, volleyball, and more. What We Built Right

  • 140+ WebP-optimized images — every photo converted from source format, lazy-loaded below the fold, sized correctly for each breakpoint. The gallery is visually rich without punishing load time.
  • Gallery lightbox with category filters — visitors can browse All, Sports, Senior Portraits, or Family photos without leaving the page. The lightbox opens full-screen with keyboard navigation and proper focus management.
  • Pricing tabs across 9 packages — three session types (Sports, Senior, Family) × three tiers (Bronze $100–200, Silver $150–225, Gold $200–300) presented in a tabbed interface. No pricing confusion, no "DM for rates."
  • Floating Book CTA — a persistent booking prompt that follows the user down the page without being intrusive. Present everywhere, never annoying.
  • JSON-LD structured data — LocalBusiness and Photographer schema on every page so search engines and AI tools can reliably extract Betsy's name, service area, services offered, and contact information.
  • GA4 analytics — Betsy can see which pages get traffic, which gallery categories get the most views, and where visitors are dropping off. Real data for real decisions.
  • Fade-in scroll animations — CSS-native scroll-driven animations that respect prefers-reduced-motion. No JavaScript animation library, no jank, no accessibility concerns.
  • Hero parallax — the landing section uses a subtle parallax layer that adds depth without overwhelming the photography that's the actual product.

The Stack Choice — Why It Matters

We built photosbyb4.com on Astro 6 deployed to Cloudflare Workers. That's not an arbitrary choice. Astro ships zero JavaScript by default — the pages are rendered server-side and delivered as fast, static HTML. Cloudflare Workers run at the edge, meaning the site loads from a server geographically close to the visitor no matter where they are. For a local photographer competing on search, every millisecond of page load speed is a ranking factor. We built the fastest version of this site that's possible.

The Gallery — 140+ Images, Zero Compromises

A photography website lives and dies by its gallery. We built the gallery component from scratch with a few non-negotiable requirements: fast load time, no layout shift, filterable by category, and lightbox viewing that doesn't steal focus from the photos.

  • WebP conversion: every source image converted to WebP format at the appropriate size for each breakpoint. Smaller files, faster loads, same visual quality
  • Lazy loading: only images in the viewport load on arrival. Scroll-based loading means the initial page weight is a fraction of the full gallery
  • Category tabs: Family (45), Sports (65), Senior Portraits (25), Behind the Lens (8) — filterable without page reload
  • Lightbox: click any photo to open full-screen. Previous/next navigation. Click outside or press Escape to close. Keyboard accessible throughout

07How This Works

Aspire doesn't hand clients a template and call it done. Every site goes through a structured build process that ensures the finished product is grounded in the business, not just the design trend of the month. Here's how we worked with Betsy from first conversation to live site.

Phase 1
Discovery
We learn the business before we touch a design file. Who are Betsy's clients? Which session types drive the most revenue? What does booking friction look like today? What do competitors do that she doesn't, and vice versa? The answers shape every decision downstream.
Phase 2
Brand & Strategy
Site architecture, content hierarchy, and visual direction locked before any code is written. For photosbyb4.com, the central decision was gallery-first: the work is the proof, so the gallery had to be fast, beautiful, and easy to navigate by category. Pricing transparency was a deliberate strategy — most photographers hide rates, which creates inquiry friction. We published them.
Phase 3
Build
Five pages built in Astro 6, deployed to Cloudflare Workers. Gallery lightbox built from scratch. Pricing tab component built from scratch. 140+ images batch-processed to WebP. JSON-LD schema written for every page. GA4 integrated. Scroll animations wired. Every page tested at 375px, 768px, 1024px, and 1440px. Accessibility checked: alt text on every image, keyboard navigation throughout, no empty buttons.
Phase 4
Review
Betsy reviewed the studio build — the internal preview URL where she could see the live site before it went public. Feedback was gathered and applied in one focused revision pass. No phone-tag, no email chains, no "can you send me a screenshot" — she saw the real thing, in the browser, on her phone and desktop.
Phase 5
Live
DNS cutover to photosbyb4.com. Deploy validation confirmed: 200 status codes across all pages, no broken links, no .workers.dev URLs in the HTML, all images resolving. Sitemap submitted. GA4 receiving live data. Site is actively indexed and discoverable. Betsy's work is now findable by every parent, athlete, and family searching for a photographer in Delaware, Ohio.
