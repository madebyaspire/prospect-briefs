01The Honest Picture

05The Future of Search

Search is changing fast. Google’s AI Overviews, voice assistants, and conversational search are reshaping how people find local services. For a mobile grooming business, this shift is actually an advantage—if you are prepared for it.

What AI Search Looks For

  • E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) — Google wants to recommend businesses with demonstrated expertise. For Zoomin Groomin, this means showcasing groomer credentials, certifications, and the brand’s training program
  • Structured data / Schema markup — AI search pulls structured information about your services, pricing, service area, hours, and reviews. Currently, the Zoomin Groomin location page has no visible LocalBusiness, Service, or Review schema
  • Conversational content — Voice search queries are longer and more natural: “Who does mobile dog grooming in Delaware Ohio?” Pages that answer these questions directly are more likely to be surfaced
  • Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) — AI search cross-references your business info across directories. Inconsistencies hurt

What Is Missing

  • No schema markup on the location page for LocalBusiness, PetGroomer, or Service types
  • No FAQ content structured for voice search or featured snippets
  • No “About Your Groomer” page for the local territory—which would signal experience and expertise to AI evaluators
  • Limited content depth on the location page—most text is identical to other Zoomin Groomin locations, which means Google may see it as duplicate content rather than unique local authority
  • No video content embedded on the local page (despite the brand having video assets)
Voice search example: “Hey Google, find a mobile dog groomer near Delaware, Ohio.” Right now, Zoomin Groomin likely does not appear in that result. With proper schema, local content, and an optimized GBP, it would.
