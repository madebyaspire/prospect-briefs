01Kayla's Story

Some things take time to become what they're meant to be. Kayla Haddad spent years building her clientele as Kayla's Hair Studio — developing her craft, earning trust chair by chair, growing a book of loyal clients who came back not just for the cut but for the experience she created. When the time came to move into a real space at 27 West Central in Delaware, Ohio, she didn't just open a salon. She started over with intention.

The name Patina says everything. It's the quality that develops on brass and leather and marble over time — not a veneer, not a gloss, but genuine character earned through use. It's the exact metaphor for what Kayla has built: a salon run by four talented stylists, each with their own specialty and following, housed in a space designed to feel like an escape. Arched mirrors. Brass hardware. Warm light. The kind of place that feels expensive because the care behind every detail is real.

Aspire's job was to make the digital presence match that standard. Not a template. Not a generic salon page. A brand with teeth — one that makes a potential new client feel, before they've ever walked through the door, that this place is different. The work started with a name, a color story, and a belief that the right brand built right from day one changes everything about how a new business grows.

The name "Patina" isn't accidental. It's a brand philosophy embedded in a single word — the idea that beauty deepens with time, that craft is earned, and that a salon worth visiting is built with the same deliberateness as the aesthetic it creates. Every decision we made, from the espresso-and-brass palette to the arch mark in the logo, was grounded in that meaning.

Four stylists with four distinct skill sets means four separate pools of search intent — and the Aspire site is structured to capture all of them.

05The Future of Search

The way people find local businesses is changing faster than most business owners realize. Google's AI Overviews now appear above organic results for service searches. ChatGPT and Perplexity are being used to answer "what's a good salon near me" questions. Voice assistants answer booking questions on the spot. None of these AI systems can recommend a business they can't find and verify.

Right now, Patina has zero AI search presence. No GBP, no indexed pages, no structured data. A potential client asking an AI assistant to recommend a balayage specialist in Delaware, Ohio would not be directed to Patina — because there's nothing to find. The Aspire build closes every one of these gaps by design.

What AI Search Looks For

  • Google Business Profile verification: The single most important signal for local AI search. Without it, a business simply doesn't exist in most AI recommendation systems.
  • E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust): Stylist bios with real credentials, portfolio images, and social links establish the expertise AI models use to validate recommendations.
  • Structured data (Schema.org): LocalBusiness schema tells search engines exactly what Patina is, where it is, and what services it offers. Person schema for each stylist extends that signal per team member.
  • Review velocity: AI systems weight recency. A new salon that launches with a GBP strategy and collects its first 20 reviews in 60 days ranks faster than one that passively waits.
  • Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone): Every directory listing must match exactly. We build this consistency in from day one.

What the Aspire Build Includes

  • LocalBusiness schema on the homepage and contact page — salon name, address, hours, phone, service category
  • Person schema on each of the four stylist pages — name, Instagram handle, specialties, booking URL
  • Salon category schema for service types (hair extensions, color treatment, keratin)
  • GBP launch day strategy — profile setup, first photos, initial review ask sequence built into the handoff
  • Instagram integration — connecting @kaylahaddadhair and individual stylist handles to the site for social proof signals
  • OpenGraph and Twitter Card metadata on every page — so every share looks professional and builds brand recognition
Starting from zero is actually an advantage — once. A business that builds its digital foundation correctly from day one doesn't have to spend years correcting legacy SEO mistakes, inconsistent NAP data, or outdated schema. Patina has one shot to do this right from launch. The Aspire build takes full advantage of that window.
