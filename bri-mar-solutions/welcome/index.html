Why Erosion Control Matters More Than Ever

Erosion control isn't optional — it's legally mandated. The EPA's NPDES Phase II regulations require every construction site disturbing one or more acres to have a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) with proper Best Management Practices (BMPs) in place. In Delaware County specifically, the DESC program adds another layer of oversight with its own permit applications, inspections, and surety requirements.

As Central Ohio's building boom continues — and environmental regulations tighten — the demand for professional erosion control services isn't going to slow down. It's going to accelerate. Understanding the DESC Regulatory Landscape

The Delaware County Engineer's Office administers one of the most rigorous erosion control programs in the state. The DESC program requires permits for virtually all construction activity that disturbs land. Here's what that means in practice:

  • General DESC Permits are required for any subdivision or commercial development. These require a full erosion and sediment control plan, surety bonds, and ongoing inspection compliance throughout the construction process.
  • Individual Lot DESC Permits are required for single-lot residential construction. Even building one house requires proper BMPs — construction entrance, silt fencing, inlet protection, and temporary seeding.
  • Stormwater administration staff from the Engineer's Office inspect active construction sites to ensure surface waters are protected from sediment and runoff. Failed inspections mean work stoppages, fines, and delays.
  • Surety requirements mean builders must post financial guarantees for erosion control. Having a reliable subcontractor who gets it right the first time directly reduces the builder's financial exposure.

This regulatory environment creates a permanent, growing demand for professional erosion control services. As Delaware County continues to grow — and it shows no signs of slowing — every new permit issued is potential work for companies like Bri-Mar. The question isn't whether the work exists. It's whether builders can find you when they need you.

The bottom line: Bri-Mar Solutions operates in a high-demand, regulation-driven market in the fastest-growing county in Ohio. The work quality is there — employees praise the company. The market opportunity is there — new construction is everywhere. The one gap is visibility: when a builder Googles "erosion control Delaware Ohio," Bri-Mar doesn't show up.
