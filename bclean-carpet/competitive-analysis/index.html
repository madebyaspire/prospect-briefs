Spinza deep dive — the hybrid pattern

Jaime flagged this Webflow template — and she's right to. Spinza earns its own section because it delivers the things the other picks only hint at: embedded booking in the hero, a beautifully staged "What We Offer" section, and a vertical scrolling testimonial wall. Here are the specific patterns we adapt for Bryan.

Why Spinza earns a section

Built to convert — without looking like a coupon

Every top-performing pattern we wanted — embedded hero form (Zerorez), stats as trust (UltraClean), editorial product photography (COIT), premium dark palette (Option B) — Spinza ships all of them in one template. It's what we were recommending, already executed.

We adapt three specific elements for Bryan: the embedded hero booking form, the "What We Offer" presentation pattern, and the vertical scrolling testimonial wall. Plus the scroll-animation language throughout — reveals, staggers, parallax.

View Spinza live ↗

Pattern 01 · What We Offer

Editorial service reveal

Centered pill label ("What We Offer"), confident two-line headline ("Simple Services, Reliable Care"), then a large editorial photo against a solid-color backdrop. Each service scrolls in with a staggered reveal — headline first, then photo fades up, then the service grid stacks in.

It's the same idea as a magazine spread: big declarative statement, then the proof. Feels curated, not sales-y.

Bryan's adaptation "What B Clean does" headline, then a close-up hero shot of fresh-cleaned carpet texture (or a before/after pair) on Bryan's teal backdrop. Four service cards underneath: Carpet · Upholstery · Tile & Grout · Pet Treatment — each with its own photo + instant price range.
Pattern 02 · Vertical testimonial wall

Sticky panel + scrolling reviews

Left side: a sticky panel with the headline ("Real Stories from Our Happy Customers"), CTA button, and stat card (2400+ Happy Customers with profile pics). Right side: three vertical columns of testimonial cards scrolling at different speeds — a living wall of social proof that moves while you read.

Way stronger than a static grid of testimonials. It makes the volume of reviews feel like a volume of reviews.

Bryan's adaptation Left sticky panel: "96 five-star reviews and counting" + "Read them all on Google" CTA + stat card. Right columns: real B Clean reviews scrolling — Kelli, Erika, Krystn, Andrew, Jesse. Star ratings visible. Dark background so Bryan's teal accent glows.
~

And the scroll animations

Spinza uses reveal-on-scroll throughout — headlines fade up, photos parallax gently, stats count up as they enter the viewport, testimonial columns scroll at offset speeds. It's never flashy — just enough motion to feel alive and premium. Our scroll-animations skill + GSAP ScrollTrigger skill cover exactly these patterns. We'd match Spinza's motion vocabulary beat-for-beat.

05

Best practices — what every top site does

Patterns that show up at every high-performing carpet-cleaning site we reviewed. These aren't opinions — these are consistent conversion drivers across the category.

01

Phone + CTA visible without scrolling

Every top site, no exception. Phone number in the top bar, primary CTA button in the hero. Friction-free contact is non-negotiable.

02

One clear differentiated headline

Never "carpet cleaning in [city]." Always a reason they're different — legacy (COIT), method (Zerorez), values. Bryan's is second-generation family expertise.

03

Zip code or instant-quote widget

Reduces friction from "visit" to "lead." Zerorez leads with it. We'd adapt for B Clean — "Get a Quote" with zip + service selector.

04

Social proof in first screen

Star rating, review count, or testimonial card. 90% of consumers read reviews before buying. Bryan has 96 five-star Google reviews — this belongs above the fold.

05

Founder story prominently featured

Confidence driver, especially for family businesses. Bryan's grandfather-founded-KE-WA-PA story is a genuine moat no competitor has.

06

Certifications as visual badges

IICRC, BBB, awards displayed as icons near the hero — not buried on a sub-page. Bryan's IICRC CCT + TMF + 2026 Quality Business Award deserve prominence.

07

Real testimonials with names

Not stock. Stars + first name + location. Bryan's real reviews ("Pet stains? What pet stains?!") read like friends talking — that's gold.

08

Before/after photos or video

Highest-converting content in the category. Bryan has these — they just need to be featured, not hidden. Time-lapse video of a real job would crush it.

09

Transparent pricing page

Almost no competitor does this. Bryan already has transparent pricing — it's an unfair advantage that should be featured, not hidden behind a "Contact Us."

10

Service-area SEO pages

Separate page per service area (Westerville, Dublin, Powell, Lewis Center, New Albany, Gahanna, Worthington) drives local SEO. Each page lightly localized.

11

FAQ for trust + SEO

Preempts objections (drying time, chemicals, pet safety). Bryan's existing FAQ content is great — just needs proper design treatment.

12

Fast load, minimal animation

Premium without lag. We'll ship WebP/AVIF images, lazy-load below the fold, self-host fonts. Target <2s LCP, 100 Lighthouse mobile.

What we mimic vs. what we avoid

The direct comparison — the specific patterns we steal from the best, and the traps we steer clear of.

Mimic

  • Zerorez's zip-code CTA in the hero
  • COIT's legacy-first editorial headline
  • UltraClean's photo collage + floating stats card
  • Clean Partners' floating testimonial overlay
  • Chem-Dry's video hero (before/after)
  • Transparent pricing as a featured page
  • Founder story with real Bryan photo, not stock
  • Certification badges in the hero area
  • Service-area pages for local SEO

Avoid

  • Discount-led headlines ("$75 off" cheapens the brand)
  • Generic blue + yellow palette (every competitor uses it)
  • Cluttered top bars with 6+ trust badges at once
  • Stock photos of smiling families on clean carpet
  • "We clean carpets in Westerville" headlines
  • Loud promo banners that scream desperation
  • Cartoon illustrations (feels friendly-cheap)
  • Services buried behind dropdown-only nav
  • Contact info hidden below the fold

10

Three directions — palette + type, together

All three keep Bryan's teal as the brand anchor. Each one pairs a palette with type that either mirrors the rounded geometric character of Bryan's logo (Outfit, Recoleta) or complements it with intentional contrast (Clash Display). None of these are our usual Playfair + Montserrat + DM Sans — we're picking fonts that feel his, not ours. Every pairing is checked for WCAG AA contrast and optimized for performance.

ADA / WCAG 2.1
AA minimum, AAA where possible

4.5:1 body contrast, 3:1 for large text. Teal #00A8B0 on light backgrounds is accent-only (2.87:1) — CTAs on light palettes use deep teal #0A4F5C (9:1+).

SEO / Performance
Self-hosted variable fonts

One or two font families per direction. Preloaded + font-display: swap. Target <80KB total font weight. Google Fonts / Fontshare both mirrored locally for privacy + speed.

Readability
Large x-heights, open apertures

All body fonts chosen for legibility at 15–16px. Display fonts restricted to 28px+ where they shine. No hairline weights below 16px. Dyslexia-friendly spacing.

The direction — locked

After weighing all three, the answer is a hybrid — Option C as the base (closest to Bryan's current brand), Option A's editorial fonts where they earn it, and Option B's drama reserved for hero, testimonials, and final CTA. One site, three moods, one coherent brand.

★ Locked Direction

Option C base · Outfit + Recoleta type · dark sections where they earn drama

The whole site isn't dark — we alternate. Light sections for the content people need to read (services, pricing, FAQ). Dark sections where atmosphere matters (hero, testimonials, final CTA). Bryan's teal is the bright beat that carries across both tones.

From Option A

Editorial feel

Recoleta italic appears on the About page, founder story, and testimonial intros. Magazine-like warmth without going full serif. Adds emotion exactly where it belongs.

From Option B

Dark drama

Hero, testimonial wall, and final CTA use the dark charcoal base. Against dark, Bryan's teal glows and CTAs naturally pass AA contrast. Drama reserved for where it converts.

From Option C

Clean base

Warm off-white as the primary background. Deep teal #0A4F5C as the anchor, Bryan's teal as the bright accent. Outfit as the primary typeface — mirrors his logo, loads fast.
