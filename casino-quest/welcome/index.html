Casino Quest is one of the most genuinely original businesses we've audited this year. Most "dealer schools" are dealer schools. Most "Vegas tourist activities" are bus tours and helicopter rides. You operate a category of one — a working casino floor where tourists learn craps before their bachelor party, where future dealers learn the same craft from the same instructors, and where a media empire (YouTube, Patreon, podcast, app) gets shot live every week. That kind of vertical integration is hard to assemble and almost impossible to fake.

The reputation reflects it. 4.9 stars on Google, Yelp, AND TripAdvisor — perfect ratings across all three platforms simultaneously — is rare even for established operators. Add 3,445 Patreon members and a YouTube clip with over a million views (the one that triggered the Fashion Show Mall expansion call), and the social-proof flywheel is already turning. Tourists arrive in Vegas already knowing your name; locals send their kids to CEG to start a dealer career.

The website is the single piece that hasn't kept up. The current site is built on Wix — which was the right tool for getting the brand off the ground, but it's now the bottleneck. The homepage forces a tourist looking for a Friday-night clinic and a 22-year-old looking for a dealer-school placement to compete for the same hero. The booking widgets are Wix-native and don't talk to your CRM. The site can't host the depth of content (manuals, strategy guides, app onboarding, dealer placements) that the brand has actually produced. The brand looks bigger on YouTube than it does on its own homepage — and that's the gap.

Where the Gaps Are

!
Two businesses, one homepage

A bachelor-party tourist and a 22-year-old job seeker need different paths. Right now they hit the same hero and the same nav. Splitting the flow could lift booking conversion 30%+ on each side.

!
Wix ceiling on speed + SEO

Wix bundles ~1MB of framework JS before your content loads. AI assistants and Google's mobile crawler both penalise that. A custom build cuts page weight 60%+ and unlocks schema you can't add today.

!
No central content + community hub

Patreon, YouTube, blog, app, and podcast each live on their own platform. Visitors who arrive from one rarely find the rest. A unified hub on your domain keeps email-list ownership in your hands.

The Vegas Search Window

Vegas search demand is anti-cyclical to most local-business markets: peak booking happens 6–8 weeks before peak Vegas traffic. That means tourists in March/April are pre-booking lessons for May–June trips, August/September pre-books for fall, and December pre-books for New Year's. A site rebuilt in the next 60 days lands ahead of the summer pre-booking window — when every dollar of organic visibility compounds across channel attribution.

AI can't read your story

The Fix
01

Schema-marked authority blocks

Founders, founding date, instructor credentials, review counts, location, and services — all in structured data on every page. AI assistants stop guessing and start citing.

↑

Your blog isn't built for citation

"David's Blog" on cqnexus.com has the right content — strategy, dealer-side perspective, Vegas insider takes — but the markup, headings, and Q&A format aren't structured for AI to extract clean answers. The expertise is there; the format isn't.

The Fix
02

FAQ + HowTo retrofit

Every existing post gets structured FAQ and HowTo blocks. Same content, AI-citable wrapper. ChatGPT starts citing David Noll's takes by name — without writing a single new article.

↑

Two URLs, divided authority

Authority is split between casinoquest.biz, cqnexus.com, shopcasinoquest.com, and the Patreon/YouTube channels. AI ranks consolidated authority. You're feeding signal into four buckets.

The Fix
03

Hub + spoke domain strategy

One canonical home (a clean .com), with the others rerouted as subdomains or properly canonicalised paths. Suddenly AI sees one operator the size of all four — not four small ones.

Right now, Casino Quest is invisible to AI for its biggest opportunity — "where can I learn craps before my Vegas trip" is a question no other operator can answer. We make sure ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini all know that.
